Chapter 1
Framing the Problem

“Reality almost always transcends its perceptual depiction.”











Traditionally, in a book such as this, the very first page of the very first chapter is dedicated to a prayer recitation, or an introduction of sorts that underlines the book’s legitimacy to the people that it is meant to address. There’s a description of God’s qualities that a human should imbibe, there’s a mention of the cosmic lineage of the mouthpiece responsible for the story behind the book, or there’s a list of incantations to be said before beginning anything to demonstrate the orator’s respect for the Almighty.

However, history is proof that this approach does not work. Even still, look at the number on this page. It surely isn’t the first page. So then, why am I rallying against something I myself have just done? Am I hypocritical, or did I just want to waste a hundred pages just talking about my special self?

The answer is neither. The previous books that were written, didn’t just contain banal salutations, and pointless incantations – certainly not in the very beginning. The authors of the book made God easily accessible by providing you with the most important information in the very beginning. Practicing religious friends of mine have even scoffed at me for even thinking of reading a scripture, any scripture, in its entirety. What a weird thing to discourage. Why wouldn’t you want to read the complete theory of everything?

One answer, and one alone – it’s boring; and with good reason too – half the stuff in the book is not meant for a regular person to comprehend. What could a person in the 2nd century CE have done after reading a prophecy for the 21st century? What use did a slave have for laws that governed the master? What was the point of mentioning the bloodline of Abraham repeatedly?

It didn’t matter then, if you started the book with Om, Ik Onkar, Bismillahirrahmanirrahim or Our Father – they have all been lead to the same demise – mechanical performance of outdated rituals in times of socio-politico-cultural decadence which is almost always coupled with an impending economic downfall .

Ordinarily, I wouldn’t even begin a conversation with the impending, but definitive apocalypse, much less the only book I will ever write – but if not here then where, and if not now then when? If I don’t have the courage to talk about the Rapture in a book that nobody has read, and will read for a considerable amount of time, how can I be entirely honest in the only book I will ever write?

It is a very famous quotation that “all roads to Hell are paved with good intentions.” I don’t doubt that – not in the slightest. I do, however, wonder and worry tremendously, about the degradation and the degeneration of individuals in particular, and societies at large, that occurs several generations after any revolution has triumphed, and slowly died down.

What I have pondered over, and the conclusions that I then arrived at, would be the subject of another book, and as such, not comprehensible to a major subset of human population. Despite that, what I can, and am permitted to, write about, is something much more substantial, that will help humans renew their covenant with God in a way that they understand best, and are most comfortable with.

What I am really proposing here, in indirect terms, is the formulation of a new religion – because that, despite what you may have been led to believe by your volition or force, is the need of this time. To get to the point where I have wanted this movement to have gotten, I see only two options with high chances of success.

The first one being the easiest – tyranny. History is proof that you can have the best intentions at heart, and believe that you have God’s support, yet descend into tyranny when tested against the currents of the time. On one hand, forced conversions are a very effective way to bring all the people together – regardless of their customs, previous beliefs and existing understanding of life – into a single entity so that an outward appearance of unity can be seen; but that’s just it – it is just the appearance of unity, not an organically developed harmony.

I could slap the modern sticker of diversity on it, and to counter them, you could propose a plethora of ways in which forced conversions of a subset of people into a new, unfamiliar ideology is – all in all, just the most dumb idea in the world. I already condemn it, so any further discussion would be a waste of valuable space, and will lead entities to find loopholes if I propose a list of any sort.

As such, these radical entities will let themselves believe that any list of reasons I give to discourage any negative act, will be exhaustive and an end unto itself; and that any other reason that I will most definitely have not mentioned will count towards the encouragement of said negative act. Those entities are the scum of the earth, and should be treated as such. Such entities will never find a place in Heaven, and it will have been nobody’s fault, but theirs.

Another option, the very unlikely, almost impossible option is for myself, to perfect the application of this knowledge that I am about to impart to you, the reader, to such an extent that I achieve for myself the limit of what is possible for a person with this knowledge – and put it to good use.

“Therefore thy gates shall be open continually; they shall not be shut day nor night; that men may bring unto thee the forces of the Gentiles, and that their kings may be brought.”
(Isaiah 60:11)

To achieve harmony after eradicating antiquated belief systems, I will then need to fill up the power vacuum that the destruction of said belief system will leave in its wake.

Now, in this case, because the ideals and beliefs that needed to be replaced were humanistic, and non-divine since their very conception – the only rational approach is to replace them with revised Divine ideals; and because we are inherently selfish and ego-centric, we would also like to know what’s in this ideal for our own puny selves that we should restructure our entire individual lives, focus our cultural and technological developments, and our economic resources towards the development of said ideal.

To that end, I would like to present the ideal of a life well lived, of a fantasy properly fulfilled, of acquisition of worthwhile knowledge and the harnessing of its innate potential – all in all, of time well spent .

If that doesn’t sound enticing to you, then you should definitely read ahead, and if it does, then I hope you are already leaning forward in your seat – intrigued at the very least.

In that regard, this being the first chapter, is called “Framing the Problem.” You could see this title from the point of view of Artificial Intelligence wherein to describe a model world, some axioms are simply stated so that other, general problems may be then solved, given those assumptions.

You could also see it as me, asking God, about where did it all go wrong? Why are we in such a state of affairs? Old questions about life’s sufferings and inevitable death notwithstanding, what the fuck is life?

I have stated this in the preface to Alethia, but now that I have told you what has been on my mind for most of my life, I would like to rephrase what the two books in Scova really depict. Alethia answers the question – what the fuck life is; following that, Neeti gives you the answer to – how the fuck do I live?

Sure enough, being born in the spacetime that you were born in, you would not have settled for a 20 something person giving you advice on how to live life. Sure, you could’ve heard him all through his wild speculations about what the world is, as long as you did not have to commit anything personally; but to take his advice on something he hasn’t personally experienced? Yeah, big no to that.

Would you have heard a 30 something person advise you on the same? Probably; but by then I would’ve already committed myself to my own life, because what level of a hypocritical coward would I be if I never tried and failed for myself at living a life while peacefully coexisting with my close companions anyway?

“In whom also we have obtained an inheritance, being predestinated according to the purpose of him who worketh all things after the counsel of his own will: That we should be to the praise of his glory, who first trusted in Christ.”

(Ephesians 1:11-12)


As God planned it, the knowledge narrated and imparted to me would be revealed to the world around the time I was a 40 year old. That’s probably the right time to give someone solid life advice – because then you know it has come from personal experience that is wilfully acquired, and not from the blind chase of some unrealistic ideal.

As is most often the case with inexperienced people, they are more than likely to fight tooth and nail for an ideology about the conception of which they have no idea, and in whose development, they are seldom involved. That, to me, goes to the very heart of the pathology of ideological possession. To quote from an interview, given by one of the few people whose articulation and resolve I have truly come to respect, Jordan B Peterson, on the subject of a purely ideological conversation:

“I am not hearing what you think, I am hearing how you are able to present the ideology you were taught; and it’s not that interesting –because when you do that, I don’t get to know anything about you.

I could then replace you with someone else with the same ideological leanings, and that means that you’re not here.

It’s not pleasant – because you are not integrating the specifics of your personal experience with what you’ve been taught, to synthesise something that is genuine and surprising and highly engaging in the narrative sense as a consequence.

And it’s not good, because then, why have a conversation? I already know where you stand on things.”

This, to me, has been THE most enervating aspect of having been born in this specific spacetime; because, for a very long time, and especially more so after the Summer of 2017, I had only wanted to have genuine, heartfelt, and empathetic conversations with, really just, everyone. I talked to people directly on this subject, mentioned it off and on in texts, and even made it my social media bio for a very long time: “Let’s have a conversation, not a collective monologue.”

To me, it was just the most vibrant philosophy to be engaged with for an entire lifetime – have truly honest conversations without pandering or societal pressure of political correctness, out of pure curiosity and without malice of any sorts.

Sure enough, as it was written over two millennia ago, my life had to play out like clockwork, even though I would almost always have no idea about it happening beforehand.

“But first must he suffer many things,

and be rejected of this generation.”

(Luke 17:25)

Even if it was written in scripture, that did not necessarily mean that I couldn’t find reasons for their occurrence. I was deeply saddened when I was first led to read this verse, angry even, but mostly disappointed – for I had much to offer, and no takers for this knowledge in a world where the validity of your knowledge was judged by the number of people already talking about it.

Surely, nothing new under the sun could be explored and shared under such a paradigm. According to my best judgment, I had something entirely novel, something at the level which nobody on the planet during my time could have possibly claimed – well, maybe the inventors of the internet.

I would gladly share the stage with them.

So then I found myself with two options again, this time my vision was focused on my personal sphere of influence, and not the world at large. The first choice was for me to keep hankering friends, and family, and any and all persons I could find, and try to force them to find out for themselves what I had truly found – to find out if people, who I had previously thought of as being better than me in most aspects, could replicate something that I had in an even better fashion.

The second option was to could explore the limits of the application of this knowledge that had been freely imparted to me, and use it in the way I knew best, in a way that exceeded imagination, in a way that fulfilled the purpose I was built for.

I tried the former approach for a very long time. It was exhausting, and to be very honest, demeaning. This was the word of God, and I was going around like a street urchin crying out loud for acceptance.

“Give not that which is holy unto the dogs, neither cast ye your pearls before swine, lest they trample them under their feet,

and turn again and rend you.”

(Matthew 7:6)

I did not like it, and I most certainly did not like who I was going to become if I were to continue down that path. I would especially not have liked it when my social bubble would surely expand, to include the entire world as it were.

So I bowed my head down, and trusted that as every dog must have its day, I too, was to have mine. I did have an advantage – I was told the exact date of the very day that would mark the inflection point in my own life, and the lives of everyone I was going to affect in the process – May 9th, 2032 CE.

Not many people get that kind of reassurance, but those people aren’t born with a divine ordained purpose now, are they ?

Naturally then, I started to mind my own life, and skewed away from trying to influence other people before I was in a position, spiritually, and physically, to nudge people in a direction that helped them in bringing about real change, in themselves, and in their own bubbles.

I stopped hoping for those honest, eclectic conversations with strangers and casual acquaintances – and started to develop deeper connections with those whom I intuitively knew were to play definitive roles in my life.

“He is despised and rejected of men; a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief: and we hid as it were our faces from him; he was despised, and we esteemed him not.


Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted.

(Isaiah 53:3-7)

When you pivot to this path that I later chose, you stop thinking about the world at large, and your place in it – you start to concentrate on your own tiny existence – you truly accept where you are in life, and start to learn to be content with what you’ve been given, rather than aspire for something that has yet to come – because the future, no matter how well prophesied, is still in flux, and is therefore, yet unwritten .

So then, even though I knew what was in store for me, and the rest of the world – I had reached the end of my spiritual journey, truly content, and truly acceptant of my true self, knowing full well that God concurred too.

Having come to this point in my spiritual journey, I felt it not as a task, or a burdensome chore, rather, a moral duty – to share with the world, on my own terms, what I had truly found. I did not want to live a legendary life that I had expected in the beginning of my journey – I wanted a full life, I wanted to run, to take a break, to fall in love, to harness my true potential, and impart my knowledge to the future generation, all the while knowing full well – that this was a fantasy being properly fulfilled in real time.

Given all that background story, I believe now is the natural starting point to begin talking about the component that nudged forward this entire journey – a ritual.

To truly appreciate the purpose, the value, the ahmeeyat of this ritual to me, personally, you have to see it not as an act that is an end unto itself – you have to inquire about the thought behind the setting of this ritual, the expected and actual outcome of this ritual, the personal want for this ritual, and most importantly, the psycho-social precursors that led to the development of this ritual in the way that it did.

Imagine Gautam, back in His day, under a tree, in a serene setting – meditating. The sight of Him meditating, is as fascinating as it is picturesque, but, remember this – He didn’t get theright to sit idly by in specific positions on the first day of his journey, he had to earn it.

What you see in the paintings and sculptures of Him, is the end result of his spiritual journey, and not the beginning. To sit at the top and do nothing, you have to have climbed the tree first (metaphor). Rome was not built in a day, and neither is a Nobel prize earned overnight – so why should you get to sit idly by and chant “ Om” repeatedly and wash away all your frailties and insecurities?

If anybody “guides” you towards meditation on day 1 of your spiritual expedition, run in the opposite direction (not literally, idiot) as there is a lot of metaphorical and physical ground that needs to be covered before you are allotted the right, the permission to sit idly by with the feeling of having truly earned it.

Our actions as sentient beings, have to have meaning, and this meaning is predicated by our life’s Journey – the where, why and how of it – most importantly, the WHEN of it. Yes, the space, the characters, the mental balance – it is all important; but the most important of all these not entirely predictable factors is the time at which a particular event occurs in relation to your own personal internal narrative, as it corresponds to the events of the bubble in which you are the lead character.

I may have possibly boosted the trumped up ego of pseudo-scientists claiming to be astrologers by saying this line – but that’s a risk we’re going to have to take if we want actual progress in this ancient meta science – to the point where the information received from it benefits the receiver as well, and not just serves as a life long stunt for a conman.

So even though I may have just given a pat on the back to the field of astrology – that should not take away from the fact that I have also used the phrase, “own personal internal narrative.” What I meant to imply was that just because I happened to mention that a certain room in a certain building on a certain street in a certain city happened to trigger my cleansing – it means nothing to you.

Just because a certain someone happens to mention that a said time of the year is auspicious for a person born in the same timeframe as yourself – and that the performance of a certain set of rites and rituals during said timeframe by you, would be able to absolve you of a certain negative energy from your life – it means nothing to you without that event clearly, and coherently being a part of your own internal narrative .

You simply cannot replicate such a cleansing, that blip in spacetime without it holding a coherently originated significance in your own personal journey.

Which is why, I am not going to tell you what my ritual is, or rather – was. Some rogue radicals and ignorant mechanical tools may disregard entirely the wisdom that preceded before the revelation of the personal tale – and may very well start the tradition of replicating my personal ritual even without any prior personal connection to said act. Let those morons be. What can be done? Nothing substantial.

Rather, I would ask you to recall what your ritual is – why is it a ritual for you? What do you get out of it? Why do you keep doing it? Is it a social thing? Why always the same set of entities in the performance of this ritual? What is the thing that nudged you towards this ritual? What’s the thing that keeps nudging you to perform this ritual regularly?

The science of existence is objective in the sense that it holds empirically true for each being, in that, it requires the same subjective understanding and action from each one of us. You can probably conduct a double blind, controlled study with a random sample from the populace for this.

You can also, maybe, verify in a different experiment that they all have the potential to achieve the same level of perceptual transcendence, but you can’t guarantee they will all reach the same end state at the same time, in the same place.

The idea of similar subjectivity, in and of itself, goes against the societal model that we have thus built over the past few centuries ever since the industrial revolution began – of replication at scale. One small factor that other humans often forget to account for when they start to replicate a ritual en masse across the social strata without any regard for the individuals’ internal state of mind or physical capabilities, or general level of understanding – is that humans are not just molecular objects interacting with other molecular objects.

In a very Gestaltian fashion, it seems almost absurd to even have to mention it, the whole is greater than the sum of its parts.

Humans, being the complex adaptive systems that we are, are as such composed of “myriad individual constituents or agents that once aggregated, take on collective characteristics that are usually not manifested in, not could easily be predicted from, the properties of the individual components themselves.”

Geoffrey West, in his book “Scale”, calls this emergent behaviour, inasmuch that the prediction of such characteristics cannot be understood by simply studying the individual parts, and that you have to look at the whole to get a clearer picture.

The whole that I am referring to, is superficially different from what the author further goes on to explore in his book, but indeed, actually does validate his understanding of abstract emergent patterns in all aspects of life.

The whole that I will be talking about at length in a short while is the complete composition of the sentient complex adaptive system as allowed to be perceived to the limits of our perception, and in elevated states of consciousness.

To that end, we now have just the ticking nerve that, to me personally at least, represents the bane of every single individual’s existence, regardless of their religious leanings – rituals and routines. First, we’ll focus on rituals – specifically, the mechanical performance of any and all encoded rituals in any and all scripture – written or otherwise .

“But when ye pray, use not vain repetitions, as the heathen do: for they think that they shall be heard for their much speaking.”

(Matthew 6:7)

You see, no matter what you do to explain to the people about a ritual, or how delicately you handle the training involved in the imparting of the knowledge required for the performance of said ritual – over time, every ritual is destined to become a mechanical thing that people just do, because other people have been doing it for a while.

(Quran 2:171)

When the philosophical and psychological continuity is lost from the original encoder to the current performer of the ritual, the act itself becomes obsolete, and as such, should be considered optional, if only under the hope that some time in the future, some self-proclaimed master of perception will arise from within the collective who will breathe new life into this antiquated act.

Every act that you engage in, every word you have ever spoken, and almost every mental construct that you have had – somebody made that, and because it managed to persist and evolve to reach your spacetime, you also do it.

The world was, and is built and run by people not much smarter than yourself. The only difference is, and was, their resolve – in what they believed – it was faith – in themselves, or God – it’s all the same.

Each time you perform a certain routine, make sure that the original meaning of the ritual is reflected in each and every movement, otherwise you’re just following something mechanically, and that is not alright. In fact, that is one of the worst possible things you can do for yourself, and towards God.

  • Don’t ring the damn bell if you truly don’t understand why.
    God isn’t your peon.
  • Don’t worship the cow only because some obscure text said so.
    Find out why.
  • Don’t slaughter helpless animals in sacrifice.
    Ask why.
  • Don’t sit around a fire pit mumbling obscure chants without deciphering the meaning of each and every incantation.
    Ask why.
  • Don’t mechanically paint symbols everywhere if you’ve never really been explained the true meaning of said symbol.
    Ask why, or better yet – stare at the damn thing and find out why.
  • Don’t be afraid to peacefully interrupt, and quench your curiosity.
    Ask why.
  • Don’t be afraid to remove yourself from a ritualistic practice if you don’t full agree with it, let others ask why and explain to you, as coherently as they can – the why .
  • Don’t just ask why just because some dude from another spacetime told you so – ask yourself – why?

Don’t think that if someone is engaged in a ritual different from yours, they are in the wrong. Ask why they’re doing it in that way, and ask if they know the meaning behind it . If they don’t know the meaning, and say that they are doing it only because it is tradition to do things a certain way – feel free to belittle them. Cause a ruckus. Let the world know that they are wrong.

(Quran 2:170)

If any harm comes to you, just know that whatever their actions towards you in that moment, and whatever your actions in that moment, it will all be accounted for in front of God – not on some obscure Day of Judgement that may never come, but at night when they sleep (more on this later).

Even if He doesn’t show up physically (which He obviously will not), He is seeing it all, and if you were pure in your intensions, no real harm can come to you. He might not give you physical strength to defend yourself in that moment, but He will set you on a path that you truly deserve – one you may not have explicitly wished for, but one that He knows is best for you; and in time, if you are discerning enough, you’ll agree too.

Keep in mind: The intent has to be purely curious, not malicious.

(Quran 2:239)


It doesn’t matter which direction you pray in, or if you pray at all. Talk to God in your private moments, ask for guidance, and He will guide you in a way best suited to your current life, while accounting for the skills you can and will acquire in this life.

“But thou, when thou prayest, enter into thy closet, and when thou hast shut thy door, pray to thy Father which is in secret; and thy Father which seeth in secret shall reward thee openly.”

(Matthew 6:6)

Just because someone’s performance of a ritual is different from your performance of the same act, doesn’t mean that they’re in the wrong. Don’t ever assume that you know all that there is to know about any ritual. You never can. Just because someone is attempting a different version of the same ritual as you, doesn’t make their way of doing things wrong.

Do you know what each and every movement of the program you follow meant to the entity that first encoded it into a ritual? Does your conduct of the same ritual preserve its original meaning? Has it been altered ever so slightly to account for the time that has elapsed between the encoding of the ritual and your performance of it? If it has not changed according to the time, chances are, YOU are doing it wrong.

Don’t perform a set of movements – any ritual – without learning about its true meaning and significance. Whenever you repeat this ritual, go over what the performance of said ritual means to you, and what it meant to the person who first codified it. It is really important that You understand what your ritual means.

Others can catch up later .

Each and every ritual has to evolve as the people performing it evolve. Nothing is static in time. It all needs to keep changing or it becomes stagnant, and hence, prone to corruption. Ask yourself, and the people around you – what does the ritual mean?

Not just the symbolism around it, but what changes in the physical world can be seen during and after the performance of said ritual? What is the role of each movement, each incantation, each and every accessory used in the ritual? Are the people surrounding the performer actually affected by the ritual, or are they just passive bystanders? Is the performer special in any way for knowing how to perform this ritual? Is the performer replaceable? Has the performer gained any special skills, or does he possess any special knowledge that led him to the discovery of said ritual?

Just a gentle reminder, this performer will always have had gained some knowledge or skills for being able to correctly perform this ritual. Nobody is just “gifted” or “blessed.” If I had to practice consistently after gaining the right knowledge from the right source to perform the rituals that I now perform, you can be damn sure that nobody on this planet is simply “gifted.”

It is of prime importance that you understand what it is that you are doing, and what it is that you are experiencing. It is now simply not enough admitting that you do not understand, or that you are not capable of making your own judgement. If you can live, breathe and interact in the modern world with all it’s technology and all the information at your fingertips – you best be assured that your mind is capable of understanding all that there is to understand about life, the universe, and our role in it. I did; and I have a very average brain. It is all only a matter of resolve, and a bit of stamina.

To explain and understand the universe, we have to rely upon our own perception of it – the organic capability of which, incidentally, almost everyone has more or less at the same level. The funny thing is, that we are bound in our understanding of everything because of our finite perceptual experiences.

Our understanding is limited by the quality and quantity of information about the world that we are able to perceive. Thus, to understand the universe – or actually – our perception of it – we have to understand the nature of information first.

It is not just that we perceive in a unidirectional manner – wherein information only goes in and triggers a response. It is a feedback loop of “reflexive self-perception” – more commonly known as introspection, specifically, internal perception of the self that mirrors our understanding of the outside world. The thinking-being, who operates in such a manner, continually develops an all encompassing theory of reality as it were as this being passes through life – the theory commonly referred to as the their “mindset.”

Given that this mindset is limited to the experiences, thus perceptions, and thus the information intake, and finally, theunderstanding of a single being – the absolute validity of such a model is almost always very limited.

Now this mindset might be, in most cases, limited – but on a very high level (programmatically speaking), it conforms with the structure of the very universe that it is modelled upon. Think of it as a beginner’s tutorial version of the multi-billion user app that everybody wishes they had built. A person whose mindset appeals, even tangentially, to another being – has the potential to be the Shepherd of the Herd.

Following this logic, Alethia can be regarded as a subclass of the logic that sentient beings employ, but a superclass of all paradigms of theology that have preceded it.

“A religion, old or new, that stressed the magnificence of the Universe as revealed by modern science might be able to draw forth reserves of reverence and awe hardly tapped by the conventional faiths.”

(The God Delusion – Page 33, Richard Dawkins)

To that end, I also don’t understand any religion that exists outside the realm of science. This is, after all, the original science – the Science of Existence. It might be referred to as meta-science before and in my time, but I surely hope that it doesn’t stay that way. The science of Strings of Time; the Science of Synchronicity; the Science of Existence – the how and why we dance to the tune of His commands – is the foremost thing that we have been studying all this time (the printing press itself, was a tangential offspring of this exploration), and it is the fundamental question that most of us are trying to answer in our own struggles that we call lives.

This, by no means, is an indication to extremists to misinterpret as a disregard for all other sciences. I, myself, am a scientist by training – or at least I try to be. The reason why I failed before, is probably the reason why most of us fail in our journeys – we try to do things without God. We get so engrossed in the workings of our puny worlds, that we forget the Creator who set all things in motion and keeps everything spinning, while the whole world changes with each unit interval of time.

Just because you can’t see God, doesn’t mean He isn’t around. Can you see quarks? You can only infer their presence with telemetry. Understanding and interpreting said telemetry requires a certain amount of skill to be gained before obscure Greek symbols and complicated mathematics behind the symbols makes any sense to you. Experiments have to be carefully designed and the results have to be reproducible under similar conditions for any hypothesis to be considered valid.

Why don’t the same standards apply to the Science of Existence? Well, for one, in this field, knowledge with copious practice, is literally power. You have an alluring affect on other entities around you – and it’s very easy to be intoxicated with this power.

It is also not just a metaphorical intoxication of having people look upto you for help, and you being incharge of them – in these altered states of consciousness, even drinking plain water will give you the intoxication of the finest wine.

After my 42 Days of Freedom, I wanted this power forever. I thought I would never lose it. Once I had gained an understanding of the intricate workings of the universe – both inner and outer – I thought I could just summon the “magic” anywhere, at anytime. I was disastrously wrong. As God would have it, over the next 15 years, I would have to learn to acquire this power on my own merit without any supplements.

Of course, the way God had planned for things to happen, I did end up going back to the shitty life that I had pre-Satori. The only difference was that now I was better prepared for what would be thrown at Me – better yet, I had God by my side. If I played my cards right, applied the right knowledge in the right place and at the right time, and practiced all that I was planning on preaching to the world – I would be invincible.
